MCAT Chemistry MCQs 41–60

Q41: What is the coordination number of the central metal ion in the complex [Co(NH3)6]3+?
The complex has 6 ammonia ligands directly bonded to Co, so coordination number = 6.
Q42: In a multistep reaction, the rate-determining step is:
The slowest step limits the overall reaction rate, known as the rate-determining step.
Q43: What is the hybridization of boron in BF3?
Boron forms three sigma bonds and is trigonal planar, so sp² hybridized.
Q44: If the Ksp of AgCl is 1.8 × 10−10, what is the molar solubility of AgCl?
Let solubility = s; Ksp = s² = 1.8×10⁻¹⁰, so s = √(1.8×10⁻¹⁰) ≈ 1.34×10⁻⁵ M.
Q45: What causes boiling point elevation in a solution?
Boiling point elevation occurs due to lowered vapor pressure caused by solute particles.
Q46: A reaction with negative ΔH and positive ΔS is:
Negative enthalpy and positive entropy favor spontaneity at all temperatures (ΔG = ΔH - TΔS).
Q47: According to molecular orbital theory, the bond order of O2 molecule is:
Oxygen has 8 bonding and 4 antibonding electrons, bond order = (8-4)/2 = 2.
Q48: Hess’s Law states that:
Hess’s Law says enthalpy change depends only on initial and final states.
Q49: Which of the following is an example of a colloid?
Milk is a colloid, a mixture with dispersed particles larger than in solutions.
Q50: According to Arrhenius definition, acids are substances that:
Arrhenius acids increase H⁺ ion concentration in aqueous solutions.
Q51: Which compound has the highest standard enthalpy of combustion?
Ethane has a higher combustion enthalpy per mole than methane, but per C atom it varies.
Q52: Which substance is the oxidizing agent?
An oxidizing agent gains electrons and causes oxidation of another substance.
Q53: Which molecule has a net dipole moment?
Water is bent and polar; CO2 is linear and nonpolar.
Q54: The Pauli Exclusion Principle states that:
Pauli Principle restricts electrons in an atom to unique quantum states.
Q55: Which of the following is NOT a colligative property?
Color change depends on substance identity, not colligative properties.
Q56: Which process always has a positive ΔH?
Endothermic processes absorb heat, so ΔH > 0.
Q57: Which element has the highest electron affinity?
Fluorine has the highest electron affinity due to its small size and high electronegativity.
Q58: Osmotic pressure is directly proportional to:
Osmotic pressure depends on molar concentration of solute particles.
Q59: The hybridization of sulfur in SF6 is:
SF₆ is octahedral with 6 bonds, requiring sp³d² hybridization.
Q60: According to Lewis definition, an acid is:
Lewis acids accept electron pairs, Lewis bases donate them.
