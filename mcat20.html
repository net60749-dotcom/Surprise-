MCAT Chemistry MCQs 1–20

Q1: What is the oxidation state of chromium in Cr2O72-?
Explanation: Chromium is +6 in dichromate ion.
Q2: What is Avogadro’s number?
Avogadro's number is approximately 6.022 × 10²³ particles per mole.
Q3: In the ideal gas equation PV = nRT, what does 'R' stand for?
R is the universal gas constant (8.314 J/mol·K).
Q4: Which best describes an exothermic reaction?
Exothermic reactions release heat, lowering system enthalpy.
Q5: Which subatomic particle has a positive charge?
Protons have positive charge; electrons negative; neutrons neutral.
Q6: Elements in the same group have the same:
Group elements share valence electron count, causing similar chemical properties.
Q7: Which bond is formed by transfer of electrons?
Ionic bonds form by electron transfer between atoms.
Q8: What is the pH of a strongly acidic solution?
Acids have pH < 7; bases > 7; neutral = 7.
Q9: What is the molar mass of oxygen gas (O2)?
O2 molar mass = 2 × 16 = 32 g/mol.
Q10: Which factor does NOT affect reaction rate?
Color does not affect rate; others do.
Q11: What does a Lewis structure represent?
Lewis structures show valence electrons and bonds.
Q12: Role of a catalyst in a reaction?
Catalysts lower activation energy, speeding up reactions.
Q13: Negative ΔH indicates?
Negative ΔH means heat is released (exothermic).
Q14: Atomic radius trend across a period?
Radius decreases left to right due to increasing nuclear charge.
Q15: Isotopes differ in:
Isotopes have same protons but different neutrons.
Q16: Electronegativity trend across period?
Electronegativity increases across a period.
Q17: Products of neutralization?
Neutralization produces salt and water.
Q18: Which force causes water’s high boiling point?
Hydrogen bonding raises water’s boiling point.
Q19: Oxidation means:
Oxidation is loss of electrons.
Q20: Sublimation is:
Sublimation is solid directly changing to gas.
