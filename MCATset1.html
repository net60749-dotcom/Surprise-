Chemistry MCQs MCAT Set 1 (Questions 1–100)

Q1: What is the oxidation state of chromium in Cr2O72-?
Explanation: In dichromate ion, chromium has an oxidation state of +6.
Q2: What is the value of Avogadro’s number?
Explanation: Avogadro's number is 6.022 × 10²³ particles per mole.
Q3: In the ideal gas equation PV = nRT, what does 'R' represent?
Explanation: R is the universal gas constant, value 8.314 J/mol·K.
Q4: Which of the following describes an exothermic reaction?
Explanation: Exothermic reactions release heat, decreasing system enthalpy.
Q5: Which subatomic particle has a positive charge?
Explanation: Proton carries positive charge, neutron is neutral, electron negative.
Q6: Elements in the same group of the periodic table have the same:
Explanation: Group elements share the same number of valence electrons.
Q7: Which bond is formed by the transfer of electrons?
Explanation: Ionic bonds form by electron transfer creating ions.
Q8: What is the pH of a strongly acidic solution?
Explanation: Acids have pH less than 7, bases greater than 7.
Q9: The molar mass of oxygen gas (O2) is approximately:
Explanation: Oxygen gas has 2 oxygen atoms, each 16 g/mol → 32 g/mol.
Q10: Which factor does NOT affect the rate of a chemical reaction?
Explanation: Reaction rate depends on temperature, concentration, surface area; color does not.
