MCAT Chemistry MCQs 21–40

Q21: What is the oxidation state of manganese in KMnO4?
In permanganate ion (MnO₄⁻), manganese has an oxidation state of +7.
Q22: Which element has the highest electronegativity?
Fluorine is the most electronegative element.
Q23: Which is the strongest acid?
Perchloric acid (HClO₄) is the strongest among the given oxyacids.
Q24: If the rate law for a reaction is rate = k[A]2[B], what is the overall order?
Sum of exponents (2 + 1) gives the overall reaction order = 3.
Q25: The standard enthalpy of formation of an element in its standard state is:
Standard enthalpy of formation for elements in their standard state is zero by definition.
Q26: At equilibrium, the rate of the forward reaction is:
At equilibrium, forward and reverse rates are equal.
Q27: According to Le Chatelier’s principle, increasing pressure favors:
Increasing pressure favors the side with fewer gas moles.
Q28: What is the oxidation state of oxygen in hydrogen peroxide (H2O2)?
In hydrogen peroxide, oxygen has an oxidation state of -1.
Q29: The first law of thermodynamics states that energy can be:
Energy is conserved; it can only be transformed.
Q30: Which of the following is true about a catalyst?
Catalysts lower activation energy but don’t affect equilibrium constant.
Q31: Which intermolecular force primarily causes the high boiling point of water?
Hydrogen bonds cause water’s unusually high boiling point.
Q32: Reduction is defined as:
Reduction is gain of electrons.
Q33: What is the molecular geometry of water?
Water has a bent molecular geometry due to lone pairs on oxygen.
Q34: Atomic emission spectra result from:
Emission spectra come from electrons falling to lower levels releasing photons.
Q35: Molarity is defined as:
Molarity = moles of solute / liter of solution.
Q36: In Bohr’s model, electrons orbit the nucleus in:
Bohr model shows electrons in fixed orbits/energy levels.
Q37: A reaction is spontaneous if the Gibbs free energy change (ΔG) is:
Negative ΔG indicates a spontaneous reaction.
Q38: Which of the following is a strong acid?
Nitric acid (HNO₃) is a strong acid.
Q39: Covalent bonds are formed by:
Covalent bonds involve sharing electrons between atoms.
Q40: A buffer solution resists changes in:
Buffers resist pH changes by neutralizing added acids/bases.
