Chemistry MCQs MCAT Set 01 (Questions 1–100)

Q1: What is the oxidation state of chromium in Cr2O72-?
Explanation: In dichromate ion, chromium has an oxidation state of +6.
Q2: According to Boyle's Law, what happens to the volume of a gas when the pressure increases at constant temperature?
Explanation: Boyle's Law states that pressure and volume are inversely proportional at constant temperature.
Q3: What does the atomic number of an element represent?
Explanation: Atomic number equals the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom.
Q4: What type of bond involves the sharing of electron pairs between atoms?
Explanation: Covalent bonds share electron pairs between atoms.
Q5: What is the pH of a neutral solution at 25°C?
Explanation: pH 7 is neutral at 25°C, below 7 is acidic, above 7 is basic.
Q6: In the periodic table, elements in the same group are arranged in the same:
Explanation: Groups are vertical columns with elements having similar properties.
Q7: What is the role of a catalyst in a chemical reaction?
Explanation: Catalysts lower activation energy and speed up reactions without being used up.
Q8: What describes the arrangement of electrons in an atom?
Explanation: Electron configuration shows how electrons are distributed among orbitals.
Q9: What does molarity measure in a solution?
Explanation: Molarity is moles of solute divided by volume of solution in liters.
Q10: Which reaction absorbs heat from its surroundings?
Explanation: Endothermic reactions absorb heat; exothermic release heat.
