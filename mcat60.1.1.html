MCAT Chemistry MCQs 40-60

40. Which element is liquid at room temperature?
Explanation: Mercury is the only metal that is liquid at room temperature.
41. What is the oxidation state of manganese in KMnO4?
Explanation: In KMnO4, manganese has an oxidation state of +7.
42. What is the hybridization of carbon in methane (CH4)?
Explanation: Carbon in methane is sp3 hybridized as it forms four single bonds.
43. Which acid is known as oil of vitriol?
Explanation: Sulfuric acid is commonly called oil of vitriol.
44. What type of bond is formed in the nitrogen molecule (N2)?
Explanation: N2 has a triple covalent bond between the two nitrogen atoms.
45. What is the pH of a neutral solution at 25°C?
Explanation: Neutral solutions have a pH of 7 at 25°C.
46. Which gas is known as laughing gas?
Explanation: Nitrous oxide is commonly called laughing gas.
47. What is the molecular geometry of ammonia (NH3)?
Explanation: Ammonia has trigonal pyramidal molecular geometry.
48. Which element has the highest electronegativity?
Explanation: Fluorine has the highest electronegativity of all elements.
49. What is the main gas responsible for the greenhouse effect?
Explanation: Carbon dioxide is a primary greenhouse gas responsible for global warming.
50. What is the molar mass of water (H2O)?
Explanation: Molar mass of water is approximately 18 g/mol.
51. What is the major product of the reaction between ethene (C2H4) and bromine (Br2)?
Explanation: Bromine adds across the double bond in ethene forming 1,2-dibromoethane.
52. Which element has the atomic number 26?
Explanation: Iron has atomic number 26.
53. What is the main constituent of natural gas?
Explanation: Methane is the major component of natural gas.
54. Which of the following compounds is amphoteric?
Explanation: Aluminum hydroxide can react as an acid or base, making it amphoteric.
55. Which is the strongest acid?
Explanation: Hydrochloric acid is stronger than HF and acetic acid.
56. What is the catalyst used in the Haber process for ammonia synthesis?
Explanation: Iron acts as the catalyst in the Haber process.
57. Which of the following is an ester?
Explanation: Ethyl acetate is a common ester formed from acetic acid and ethanol.
